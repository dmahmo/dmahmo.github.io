Dhekra Mahmoud

Poems

Speak
I see your lips stained with silence Are you forcing them shut? I see muddied sand settled at the bottom of your mute mouth Vomit and speak. Speak, and do not claim inability I gave you, in secret, all the letters Speak, and do not claim ignorance. I taught you aloud, as the Lord taught Adam all the names Vomit and speak. Speak with the honesty of a newborn, like the first to ever speak Speak with the truth of Christ on the cross, like the last to ever speak Speak, and confess your final foolishness, your final sin, so that my blood will not be spilled like mere water upon the ground of your memory Speak, and let your vomit heal wounds that never closed Let your hands exhume virgin graves whose funerals were never completed Speak. Perhaps in a cry of uproar we may finally bury our memory Silence, your silence, my beloved is a false prayer call So speak and vomit.
