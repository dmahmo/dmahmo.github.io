Petit Papillon Pécheur
Do you want to see me? Alright! Take off from me the thin, dry white veil that is not mine. Let me rub my fingers and nails in my menstrual blood, and color my whole body with my henna… the deep red henna of my hands. Now cover me with it. Know that white is not born from me, even if I want it to be. I am barren of it. Mother… I miss your sweet nipples, firm and wet, like dew on two carnations. Feed me again… Maybe the aged nectar of your grape-flavored breast will make me drunk… and I will become one with my ethereal body. Feed me a third time… Perhaps your holy, divine cure will baptize my organs, and I will be born again through you. Feed me a fourth, a fifth, and many more times so that I may be purified… Do not hurry to wean me… For who but you, Mother, can cleanse me and make me clean? Who but you, Mother, would I accept as my god? Is there any god but you who would save me? Is there any god but you who would forgive being shared? Is there any god but you who would let me into their paradise even if I denied them? Be my god… Be my god, or return me to my first paradise, the paradise of your womb… And stand between me and the world like a white cocoon, pure and safe, through which I breathe - through you, through which I live - through you, and with which I fade away like a butterfly floating in nothingness.
Speak the Truth
I see your lips stained with silence Are you forcing them shut? I see muddied sand settled at the bottom of your mute mouth Vomit and speak. Speak, and do not claim inability I gave you, in secret, all the letters. Speak, and do not claim ignorance. I taught you aloud, as the Lord taught Adam all the names. Vomit and speak. Speak with the honesty of a newborn, like the first to ever speak Speak with the truth of Christ on the cross, like the last to ever speak Speak, and confess your final foolishness, your final sin, so that my blood will not be spilled like mere water upon the ground of your memory. Speak, and let your vomit heal wounds that never closed. Let your hands exhume virgin graves whose funerals were never completed. Speak, and... Perhaps in a cry of uproar we may finally bury our memory. Silence, your silence, my beloved is a false prayer call... So speak and vomit the Truth.
Under the Ice
Blindfold your eyes to see it- a white hill in the ice. Hold your breath to follow, panting, the rhythm of its steps in the ice. The flourishing, stiff frond of its tomb will not guide you to it. All roads that lead to it do not lead to it. The Fata Morgana will shade you; Sirena’s chants will tempt you. So strip away your deadly senses and imprison them-if you wish- in the ice. And listen closely to it in winter’s noise, with your whole body, so it will carry you to it. And ink the sky with the sap of a Christmas tree branch, its Ten Commandments. Perhaps it may not find comfort in the ice.
